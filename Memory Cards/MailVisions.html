DPD fresh, the new entity of parcel service DPD, delivers dry, fresh and frozen products in the Belux

Supply of food to private individuals and professionals in the Belux

October 1, 2020 - After thorough market research, based on the experience of classic parcel delivery and of its sister company Chronofresh, and the potential of the sector as such, DPD has decided to set up a new entity: DPD fresh. DPD fresh is a new express transport service (delivery within 24 hours) for the delivery of dry food as well as fresh and frozen products to professionals and private individuals. The rollout of this new service is accompanied by the move into a new building in Asse-Zellik, part of which is equipped with refrigeration and freezer installations for the food products. In the first phase, DPD fresh will supply 19 Brussels municipalities * and the Flemish periphery around Brussels, by 2022 the new service will be rolled out all over Belgium and Luxembourg.

What does DPD fresh offer?

“Our motto is Optimal freshness door to door”, explains Koen Verlaenen, general manager of DPD fresh Belux. “We are responsible for the collection and delivery of dry food, fresh food and frozen products from our customers and deliver them to the recipient under the most optimal conditions. For dry food this is done without refrigeration, for fresh food (vegetables, fruit, meat or fish) at temperatures between 0 and 4 ° C. All frozen food is cooled to -18 ° C. Unique to DPD fresh is the actively cooled network. This means that we actively cool ourselves, both in the depots and during transport. This saves customers the often expensive and polluting packaging to keep their goods at the right temperature. ”


Maximum freshness

Substantial investments were made in the necessary infrastructure. The depot in Asse-Zellik is equipped with cold storage and freezer cells that are monitored around the clock. For collection and delivery, DPD fresh works with roll containers, isothermal boxes and refrigerated trucks, so that the preservation of the cold chain, hygiene and food safety can be guaranteed at all times. All measures are described in detail in a Health Control Plan that is fully compliant with the very strict regulations of the Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain (FASFC). Each DPD fresh employee received extensive training in the DPD learning & development department, on both the logistics sector and everything to do with hygiene and food safety.

Addressees of DPD fresh deliveries can, just like recipients of DPD parcels, use the myDPD app, which announces their delivery up to one hour. With the app, they can also track their shipment live and change the address and / or date of the delivery up to 30 minutes before delivery.


The food industry, a sector with a lot of potential

DPD did not just take the step to the food industry. On the one hand, the new service builds on the knowledge and expertise that DPD has built up over the past decades and on the inspiring example of the French sister company Chronofresh. That has been active in France for 5 years. Chronofresh was increasingly asked by French customers to also be able to deliver in Belgium.

On the other hand, a market study commissioned by DPD in Belgium showed that the potential for new services in our country is very great. The digital evolution, reinforced by COVID-19 in recent months, has a lasting impact on consumer purchasing behavior. In addition, we attach increasing importance to sustainability and quality, also in the field of nutrition. We prefer to eat a little less, but we do have higher demands in terms of quality.

In the Belgian food sector, by far the largest industrial sector (with 20% of the total of all sectors), just over 4,000 companies together realize a turnover of 57 billion euros annually, 45% of which comes from exports. In 2019, only 1.5% of all food in Belgium was traded via online channels. It is estimated that this share could double to 3% by 2025. This estimate dates from before COVID-19 and may therefore need to be revised upwards.

PostNL starts building new parcel sorting centre near Amsterdam

PostNL has this week celebrated the start of construction work for a new parcel sorting centre north of Amsterdam as it continues to invest in capacity expansion.

The Economy Alderman for Zaanstad, Hans Krieger, and Operations Manager of PostNL Pakketten, Iris van Wees, together symbolically laid the foundation stone for the sustainable new premises at HoogTij business park in Westzaan, about 25km from central Amsterdam.  

The new Westzaan facility will help increase capacity for sorting and distributing parcels. PostNL expects to be able to process between 40,000 and 50,000 parcels there on a daily basis.

The parcel sorting centre will become operational in summer 2021 and parcels will be delivered from there to addresses in and around the municipality of Zaanstad, including Purmerend, Volendam and Beverwijk.

2 new centres in 2021

Iris van Wees, Operations Manager of PostNL Pakketten, said: ‘Covid-19 has changed consumers’ behaviour. They’re placing more and more online orders. We have responded to the rapid growth in volume by scaling up our capacity by 40%. In the run up to the holidays period, we will be continuing those efforts and investing in new locations in order to optimise the delivery process in the growing parcel market.

“We will be opening two new parcel locations in the Netherlands next year. The sustainable premises in Westzaan is one of those locations,” she added.

Marco van der Laan, director of construction firm Aan de Stegge Twello, commented: “Having previously realised other sorting centres for PostNL, we’re delighted to be able to collaborate on another attractive parcel sorting centre. The premises in Westzaan is the 14th that we’re building for PostNL. That demonstrates our mutual trust and effective collaboration.”

PostNL intends to obtain the BREEAM building sustainability certificate 'Outstanding' for this parcel sorting centre, making it the eighth PostNL parcel sorting centre with a BREAAM certificate.

For this purpose, PostNL is investing in sustainable materials, the installation of around 900 solar panels and waste heat recovery systems. In addition, the premises will be free of gas. This makes these premises one of the most sustainable buildings.

Logistics network expansion

The new PostNL location in Westzaan will be part of a network of 25 parcel sorting centres in the Netherlands and six locations in Belgium. In 2021, PostNL will open two new parcel locations in the Netherlands: this location in Westzaan and a special sorting centre for small parcels in Nieuwegein. In Belgium, a new high-tech sorting centre will be built in Willebroek, which will come into operation in 2021. These new locations will significantly expand the capacity for sorting and distributing parcels.

To Alderman Hans Krieger, PostNL’s arrival in Westzaan is very welcome: ‘Commercial space is in high demand at HoogTij. The company will create employment opportunities, which is an important criterion for the municipality in allocating commercial space. This new parcel sorting centre will create around 400 jobs - jobs that really are needed in this region and come as welcome news to Zaanstad.’
