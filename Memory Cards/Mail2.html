German online sales grow over 13% in Q3 driven by everyday goods and groceries

German e-retail continued to grow strongly between July and September, as consumers kept buying more everyday goods and groceries online and parcel deliveries continued to surge, new figures by the German e-commerce association Bevh have shown.

During the third quarter of 2020, online sales drove growth in the overall German interactive trade with a 13.3% increase to €19.3 billion, compared to the third quarter last year.

The overall interactive trade in Germany generated sales worth €19.65 billion in Q3 2020. Accordingly, online trade now accounts for 98.4% of the total interactive retail sales in the country.

Sales of physical goods continued to dominate the German e-commerce business. In contrast, digital products and services such as electronic tickets, downloads, hotel bookings, etc. slumped massively with sales of €1.7 billion including VAT in the third quarter, thus reaching only a third of the previous year's revenues (€5.1 billion including VAT).

However, due to the coronavirus-related slump during the first quarter of 2020, especially in the clothing sector, the overall growth in 2020 is still slightly below the average of the previous years in percentage terms, the association stressed. Cumulated e-commerce revenues over the first nine months of this year increased by 10.6% to €56 billion, compared to the first nine months of 2019.

"Although all shops have reopened since July, many consumers continue to rely on the resilient delivery structures of online and mail-order business," bevh’s executive director, Christoph Wenk-Fischer, said. "When comparing the sales channels, it became clear again that the internet pure players were able to attract most of the market growth in e-commerce."

Online sales of everyday goods and groceries soar, clothing sales back to normal

Top of the Q3 growth list were online sales of everyday goods which grew by 34% to €1.7 billion. They were already at the top of the growth table during the first two quarters of 2020. Online grocery sales also surged by more than half (+52.9%) to €633 million.

During the third quarter, Germans were happy to invest the money that they didn’t spend on travelling in home and garden furniture which increased sales by 19.5% to €3.2 billion. They also ordered more medicine online than on average with sales rising to €312 million (compared to €220 million in Q3 2019.)

Online sales of clothes and shoes have returned to their normal growth rate in Q3 with a 12.2% increase to €5.1 billion. Excluding shoes, online sales of clothes rose even slightly above average by €13.9 to €3.96 billion. Cumulated nine-month sales in the clothes and shoes segment rose by 7.5%, dragged down by the weak first quarter.

Leisure goods grew by 12.3% to nearly €2.3 billion. Entertainment products showed significantly weaker growth of only 6.1% to €6.1 billion. Video and music downloads, however, rose strongly by 18.7% to €717 million as they replaced cinema and concerts for many people.

Strong growth for distance-selling firms

In terms of sales channels, classic distance-selling companies registered the strongest Q3 growth of 17.3% with revenues amounting to €2.98 billion. Marketplaces increased their revenues by 13.9% to €7.97 billion.

Internet pure players just missed the €3 billion mark in terms of revenues growing by 10.9% to €2.96 billion. Online shops of traditional retailers increased revenues by only 6.7% to €2.7 billion and represent less than 14% of the overall e-commerce sales.

Double-digit overall growth forecast for 2020

Despite the slump in the first quarter, the association remains hopeful that online sales of goods could reach €80 billion (including VAT) this year. For the overall interactive trade, bevh predicts 10% growth with around €82 billion in revenues.

“Depending on how things develop over the next three months, the overall e-commerce market including services can still surpass the €90 billion mark. As soon as trips and events are booked online again, the e-commerce market with end-consumers will surely reach more than €100 billion,” Christoph Wenk-Fischer predicted.

Source: bevh, CEP-Research
